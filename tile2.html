DIRECT from ARTIST to AUDIENCE

Accessible through QR codes at gigs or shared directly by artists.

Click on a song to listen.

Problem

  • AI Theft: Music is stolen and reproduced without compensating creators.
  • Broken Streaming Model: Streams earn only cents; musicians must rely on live performance to survive.
  • Gatekeepers: Spotify, YouTube Music, and similar platforms control access, rarely surfacing local or indie acts.
  • Cultural Loss: Algorithms promote overseas artists, leaving Australia’s music scene underexposed to new listeners.

AyeeyA

Despite—or because of—their fugitive status, Aye eyA has cultivated a loyal following across Western Sydney’s youth, who see in them not just defiance, but reflection.

Solution

  • Digital EPs via QR Codes: Artists hand their music directly to fans at gigs or wherever they choose.
  • No Gatekeepers: Listeners access the music instantly—no algorithm or platform interference.
  • Support Local: Fans connect directly with emerging and independent Australian talent.

Benefits

  • Artists keep creative and financial control.
  • Fans discover authentic, local music easily.
  • Communities strengthen cultural identity through direct artist–audience connection.
  • Australia preserves its music industry against algorithmic gatekeeping.

Future Case Studies & Possibilities

Community Vinyl Revival

If you love an artist, an era or a theme or purpose, upload their songs and create an environment. The possibilities are endless.

Teasers and Takeways

Give away teasers to entice people to come to your music festival or create a takeaway with the songs (perhaps live) from the event to keep fans engaged.

A gift of Song

In the 70s we stood by the radio with a cassette player waiting for that favourite song; in the 90s people bought mp3s and made playlists for loved ones. Now you can buy songs from artists online, upload them to the app, choose your pictures, fonts and colours, and give a unique gift of music.

Cassettes

These pieces are still in progress. I’m exploring the idea that creative work doesn’t need to sit entirely online or offline. There’s a growing pull back toward the tactile—analog textures, real buttons—and I’m leaning into that.

I’ve also been experimenting with blending AI‑generated voices as background, filler, or placeholders, while keeping human voices and the contributions of fellow artists at the centre. Used well, AI can make early experimentation far more affordable. Directors and artists can test variations quickly and cheaply without assembling the full cast and crew.

Pitches to producers no longer need to be limited to written or spoken formats. They can incorporate visualisers or audio‑visualisers to communicate tone, rhythm, and atmosphere.

Once producers narrow down their choices from a wider pool of experiments, they can bring in the artists to elevate the work and shape the final connection with the audience.

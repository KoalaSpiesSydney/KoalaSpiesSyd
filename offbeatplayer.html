Problems

New Australian musicians are doing it hard. AI systems are increasingly scraping and reproducing music without compensating the creators; the streaming economy is broken, with artists earning only cents per play and forced to rely on live performance to survive; major platforms like Spotify and YouTube Music act as gatekeepers, rarely surfacing local or emerging acts; and algorithm-driven recommendations overwhelmingly favour overseas artists, leaving Australia's music scene underexposed and culturally diminished.

Aye eyA

Aye eyA has carved out a cult-level following across Western Sydney's youth — the ones who live outside the lines and don't apologise for it. Dropping onto Spotify or the big platforms would kill the whole mythos. Their power comes from being underground, hard to track down, and proudly untamed — the kind of act you hear about in alleyways, not algorithms.

Solution

  • Digital EPs via QR Codes: Artists hand their music directly to fans at gigs or wherever they choose.
  • No Gatekeepers: Listeners access the music instantly — no algorithm or platform interference.
  • Support Local: Fans connect directly with emerging and independent Australian talent.
